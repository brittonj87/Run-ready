⚖️ Weight loss basics

Aim for a modest calorie deficit — big enough to lose fat, small enough that you can still train well.

  • Protein + fiber at every meal to stay full on fewer calories.
  • Favor whole foods over packaged/processed — more volume, fewer calories.
  • Don't under-eat on hard training days — fuel the work, cut on easy/rest days instead.
  • Sleep and stress matter as much as food for sustainable loss.

🏃 Fueling for long runs

Before (1–3 hrs): easy-to-digest carbs + a little protein, low fat/fiber.

Try — oatmeal with banana, toast with honey, bagel with a little peanut butter.

During (runs over ~75–90 min): 30–60g carbs per hour, sipped regularly.

Try — sports drink, gels, chews, or dried fruit every 20–30 min.

After (within 30–60 min): carbs + protein, roughly 3:1 ratio, to refill glycogen and repair muscle.

Try — chocolate milk, a smoothie, or eggs with toast.

💧 Hydration

  • Baseline: about half your bodyweight (lbs) in ounces of water daily.
  • Add 16–24 oz for every hour of running, more in heat.
  • On long runs past 60–90 min, add electrolytes — not just water.
  • Check urine color: pale yellow is the target.

🍽️ Meal ideas

Breakfast — Greek yogurt, berries, granola, chia seeds.
Lunch — Grilled chicken or tofu bowl: rice, roasted veg, beans, olive oil.
Dinner — Salmon or lean beef, sweet potato, big side salad.
Snacks — Cottage cheese + fruit, hummus + veggies, a handful of nuts.
